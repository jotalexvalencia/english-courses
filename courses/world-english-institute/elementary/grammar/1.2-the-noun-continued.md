
# I. Grammar 1.2: The Noun (Continued)

## THE NOUN (CONTINUED)

### INTRODUCTION

**In Lesson One, we learned** `In Le-son Wan, wi lernd` **to recognize and use different kinds** `tu re-cog-naiz and yus di-fe-rent kainds` **of nouns: proper and common nouns,** `ov nauns: pro-per and co-mon nauns,` **collective nouns, concrete and abstract nouns,** `ko-lek-tiv nauns, kon-krit and abs-trakt nauns,` **and specific and non-specific nouns.** `and spe-si-fik and non-spe-si-fik nauns.`

**In this lesson, we will learn** `In dis le-son, wi wil lern` **about singular, plural, and possessive nouns.** `a-baut sing-gyu-lar, plu-ral, and po-se-siv nauns.` **But first, let us learn** `Bat ferst, let as lern` **some new words.** `som niu werds.`

### NOUNS TO LEARN

**1. "apostrophe" (noun)** `wan. "a-pos-tro-fi" (naun)` **-- a punctuation mark (')** `-- a pank-chu-ei-shon mark (')` **used to show that something** `yusd tu shou dat sam-thing` **belongs to a person, place, or thing.** `bi-longs tu a per-son, pleis, or thing.`

**Sentence: "An apostrophe is also used** `Sen-tens: "An a-pos-tro-fi is ol-so yusd` **to show that a letter** `tu shou dat a le-ter` **has been left out of a word."** `has bin left aut ov a werd."` **Example: cannot > can't.** `Eg-zam-pol: ca-not > kant.`

**2. "computer" (noun)** `tu. "kom-piu-ter" (naun)` **-- an electronic machine** `-- an e-lek-tro-nik ma-shin` **that performs high-speed mathematical calculations** `dat per-forms hai-spid ma-the-ma-ti-cal kal-kiu-lei-shons` **storing and processing information.** `sto-ring and pro-se-sing in-for-mei-shon.`

**Sentence: "Many people in America** `Sen-tens: "Me-ni pi-pol in A-me-ri-ka` **now have personal computers at home."** `nau hav per-so-nal kom-piu-ters at joum."`

**3. "consonant" (noun)** `thri. "kon-so-nant" (naun)` **-- any letter in the English alphabet** `-- e-ni le-ter in de Ing-glish al-fa-bet` **except "a," "e," "i," "o," and "u."** `ek-sept "ei," "i," "ai," "ou," and "iu."`

**Sentence: "A consonant is a sound** `Sen-tens: "A kon-so-nant is a saund` **of speech produced by using** `ov spich pro-diusd bai iu-sing` **the lips, teeth, throat, or tongue** `de lips, tith, throt, or tang` **to block the flow of air."** `tu blok de flou ov er."`

**4. "killer whale" (noun)** `for. "ki-ler weil" (naun)` **-- a very large black and white dolphin** `-- a ve-ri larch blak and wait dol-fin` **that hunts in packs** `dat hants in paks` **and feeds on large fish and seals.** `and fids on larch fish and sils.`

**Sentence: "Most killer whales are friendly** `Sen-tens: "Most ki-ler weils ar frend-li` **to human beings."** `tu hiu-man bi-ings."`

**5. "porpoise" (noun)** `faiv. "por-pois" (naun)` **-- a small, friendly marine mammal** `-- a smol, frend-li ma-rin ma-mal` **related to the whale,** `ri-lei-ted tu de weil,` **with a blunt snout** `wid a blant snaut` **and a torpedo-shaped body** `and a tor-pi-dou-sheipt bo-di` **about two meters long.** `a-baut tu mi-ters long.`

**Sentence: "Porpoises are found in seas,** `Sen-tens: "Por-poi-ses ar faund in sis,` **gulfs, and oceans throughout the world."** `galfs, and o-shons thru-aut de werld."`

**6. "relationship" (noun)** `siks. "ri-lei-shon-ship" (naun)` **-- an emotional connection** `-- an i-mo-sho-nal ko-nek-shon` **between two or more persons;** `bi-tuin tu or mor per-sons;` **an association; a kinship.** `an a-so-si-ei-shon; a kin-ship.`

**Sentence: "Adam and Eve entered** `Sen-tens: "A-dam and Iv en-terd` **into an honorable relationship** `in-tu an o-no-ra-bol ri-lei-shon-ship` **that was so strong and so satisfying** `dat was so strong and so sa-tis-fai-ing` **that they became as one."** `dat dei bi-keim as wan."`

**7. "vowel" (noun)** `se-ven. "vau-el" (naun)` **-- "a," "e," "i," "o," or "u."** `-- "ei," "i," "ai," "ou," or "iu."`

**Sentence: "A vowel is a sound** `Sen-tens: "A vau-el is a saund` **made when air passes freely** `meid wen er pa-ses fri-li` **through the voice box and the mouth.** `thru de vois boks and de mauth.`

### OTHER WORDS TO LEARN

**1. "to communicate" (infinitive) [verb preceded by "to"]** `wan. "tu ko-miu-ni-keit" (in-fi-ni-tiv) [verb pri-si-ded bai "tu"]` **-- to exchange or pass along thoughts;** `-- tu eks-cheinch or pas a-long thots;` **to give and receive knowledge.** `tu giv and ri-siv no-lech.`

**Sentence: "Unlike animals, human beings** `Sen-tens: "An-laik a-ni-mals, hiu-man bi-ings` **are able to communicate with God** `ar ei-bol tu ko-miu-ni-keit wid God` **and to enjoy a close relationship** `and tu en-yoi a klous ri-lei-shon-ship` **with him."** `wid him."`

**2. "cultivate" (verb)** `tu. "kal-ti-veit" (verb)` **-- to grow; to tend.** `-- tu grou; tu tend.`

**Sentence: "Adam cultivated the garden of Eden** `Sen-tens: "A-dam kal-ti-vei-ted de gar-den ov I-den` **and gathered its fruits."** `and ga-derd its fruts."`

**3. "intelligent" (adjective)** `thri. "in-te-li-yent" (a-dyek-tiv)` **-- having intelligence;** `-- ha-ving in-te-li-yens;` **having the capacity to think,** `ha-ving de ka-pa-si-ti tu think,` **learn, and understand.** `lern, and an-der-stand.`

**Sentence: "Human beings are generally** `Sen-tens: "Hiu-man bi-ings ar ye-ne-ra-li` **more intelligent than animals."** `mor in-te-li-yent dan a-ni-mals."`

**4. "irregular" (adjective)** `for. "i-re-giu-lar" (a-dyek-tiv)` **-- not conforming to the rules** `-- not kon-for-ming tu de ruls` **or to the usual pattern.** `or tu de iu-shu-al pa-tern.`

**Sentence: "Irregular nouns are nouns** `Sen-tens: "I-re-giu-lar nauns ar nauns` **that do not follow the rules** `dat du not fo-lou de ruls` **when changing from singular to plural."** `wen chein-ying from sing-giu-lar tu plu-ral."`

**5. "responsible" (adjective)** `faiv. "ris-pon-si-bol" (a-dyek-tiv)` **-- answerable; accountable;** `-- an-se-ra-bol; a-kaun-ta-bol;` **expected to give an account.** `eks-pek-ted tu giv an a-kaunt.`

**Sentence: "God has made us responsible** `Sen-tens: "God has meid as ris-pon-si-bol` **for ruling over the animals** `for ru-ling o-ver de a-ni-mals` **of the earth."** `ov de erth."`

### SINGULAR AND PLURAL NOUNS

**Look around you.** `Luk a-raund iu.` **Describe what you see.** `Des-kraib wat iu si.` **Are there two or more** `Ar der tu or mor` **of the same item?** `ov de seim ai-tem?` **If so, you will have to use** `If so, iu wil jav tu ius` **plural nouns to describe them.** `plu-ral nauns tu des-kraib dem.` **Examples: books, lamps, desks, chairs,** `Eg-zam-pols: buks, lamps, desks, chers,` **pencils, pens, boxes, pictures, clocks, waste baskets.** `pen-sils, pens, bok-ses, pik-chers, kloks, weist bas-kets.` **Sentence: "There are three chairs** `Sen-tens: "Der ar thri chers` **and one desk in my office."** `and wan desk in mai o-fis."`

**A singular noun is a noun** `A sing-giu-lar naun is a naun` **that names only one person, place, or thing.** `dat neims on-li wan per-son, pleis, or thing.`

**A plural noun is a noun** `A plu-ral naun is a naun` **that names more than one** `dat neims mor dan wan` **person, place, or thing.** `per-son, pleis, or thing.`

Singular Nouns | Plural Nouns
-------------- | ------------
**woman** `wu-man` | **women** `wi-men`
**tree** `tri` | **trees** `tris`
**truth** `truth` | **truths** `truths`
**fear** `fir` | **fears** `firs`
**party** `par-ti` | **parties** `par-tis`

**How do you make a noun plural?** `Hau du iu meik a naun plu-ral?`

**Most nouns can be made plural** `Most nauns kan bi meid plu-ral` **by simply adding "s."** `bai sim-pli a-ding "s."`

**Examples: bird--birds; flower-- flowers; tree--trees.** `Eg-zam-pols: berd--berds; flau-er--flau-ers; tri--tris.`

**However, nouns that end with "s,"** `Hau-e-ver, nauns dat end wid "s,"` **"sh," "ch," "x," or "z"** `"sh," "ch," "x," or "z"` **are made plural by adding "es."** `ar meid plu-ral bai a-ding "es."`

**Examples: glass--glasses; wish--wishes;** `Eg-zam-pols: glas--gla-ses; wish--wi-shes;` **church--churches; fox--foxes.** `church--chur-ches; fox--fok-ses.` **Sentence: "The foxes have holes** `Sen-tens: "De fok-ses hav houls` **to live in, and the birds** `tu liv in, and de berds` **have nests to live in,** `hav nests tu liv in,` **but the Son of Man** `bat de San ov Man` **has no place where he can** `has no pleis wer hi kan` **rest his head" (Luke 9:58).** `rest his hed" (Luk nain:fif-ti-eit).`

**Also, nouns that end with an "o"** `Ol-so, nauns dat end wid an "o"` **preceded by a consonant** `pri-si-ded bai a kon-so-nant` **are made plural by adding "es."** `ar meid plu-ral bai a-ding "es."`

**Examples: mosquito--mosquitoes; potato--potatoes; hero--heroes.** `Eg-zam-pols: mos-ki-to--mos-ki-tos; po-tei-to--po-tei-tos; hi-ro--hi-ros.` **Sentence: "Idaho is famous** `Sen-tens: "Ai-da-jo is fei-mos` **for its potatoes."** `for its po-tei-tos."`

**Nouns that end with a consonant** `Nauns dat end wid a kon-so-nant` **followed by a "y"** `fo-loud bai a "wai"` **are made plural by changing the "y"** `ar meid plu-ral bai chein-ying de "wai"` **to "i" and adding "es."** `tu "ai" and a-ding "es."`

**Examples: library--libraries; secretary--secretaries;** `Eg-zam-pols: lai-bre-ri--lai-bre-ris; se-kre-te-ri--se-kre-te-ris;` **baby--babies; city-- cities.** `bei-bi--bei-bis; si-ti--si-tis.` **Sentence: "Most cities have** `Sen-tens: "Most si-tis hav` **several good libraries."** `se-ve-ral gud lai-bre-ris."`

**However, when a noun ends** `Hau-e-ver, wen a naun ends` **with a vowel followed by a "y,"** `wid a vau-el fo-loud bai a "wai,"` **it is made plural** `it is meid plu-ral` **by simply adding "s."** `bai sim-pli a-ding "s."`

**Examples: journey--journeys; key--keys; toy--toys.** `Eg-zam-pols: yer-ni--yer-nis; ki--kis; toi--tois.` **Sentence: "He has nine keys** `Sen-tens: "Hi has nain kis` **in his pocket."** `in his po-ket."`

**Most nouns that end with "f"** `Most nauns dat end wid "f"` **or "fe" are made plural** `or "fe" ar meid plu-ral` **by changing the ending to "ves."** `bai chein-ying de en-ding tu "ves."`

**Example: knife--knives; leaf--leaves; wife--wives.** `Eg-zam-pol: naif--naivs; lif--livs; waif--waivs.` **Sentence: "She has twenty-three knives** `Sen-tens: "Shi has twen-ti-thri naivs` **in her kitchen."** `in her ki-chen."`

### POSSESSIVE NOUNS

**A possessive noun is a noun** `A po-se-siv naun is a naun` **that indicates ownership or relationship.** `dat in-di-keits ou-ner-ship or ri-lei-shon-ship.`

**Examples: the boy's bicycle, the girl's doll,** `Eg-zam-pols: de bois bai-si-kol, de gerls dol,` **the man's strength, the woman's beauty.** `de mans strength, de wu-mans biu-ti.`

**Most singular nouns are made possessive** `Most sing-giu-lar nauns ar meid po-se-siv` **by simply adding an apostrophe (')** `bai sim-pli a-ding an a-pos-tro-fi (')` **and an "s."** `and an "s."`

**Examples: child--child's; teacher--teacher's.** `Eg-zam-pols: chaild--chailds; ti-cher--ti-chers.` **Sentence: "This is the teacher's book."** `Sen-tens: "Dis is de ti-chers buk."`

**The apostrophe is a helpful little tool.** `De a-pos-tro-fi is a help-ful li-tol tul.` **It is used** `It is iusd`
**(a) to show possession and relationship,** `(ei) tu shou po-se-shon and ri-lei-shon-ship,`
**(b) to take the place** `(bi) tu teik de pleis` **of a missing letter in a word,** `ov a mi-sing le-ter in a werd,` **and** `and`
**(c) to form the plural** `(si) tu form de plu-ral` **of letters and numbers.** `ov le-ters and nam-bers.`

**Think how much more difficult** `Think hau mach mor di-fi-kult` **the English language would be** `de Ing-glish lang-guich wud bi` **without it.** `wi-daut it.` **Instead of saying "God's world,"** `Ins-ted ov sei-ing "Gods werld,"` **we would have to say** `wi wud hav tu sei` **"the world that belongs to God."** `"de werld dat bi-longs tu God."`

**Examples: "This is the carpenter's hammer."** `Eg-zam-pols: "Dis is de kar-pen-ters ha-mer."` **"The nation's honor must be respected."** `"De nei-shons o-nor mast bi ris-pek-ted."` **"That's right." "It's great to be alive!"** `"Dats rait." "Its greit tu bi a-laiv!"` **"There are two a's** `"Der ar tu eis` **in the word 'altar.'"** `in de werd 'ol-tar.'"`

**Plural nouns that end in "s" or "es"** `Plu-ral nauns dat end in "s" or "es"` **are made possessive by adding an apostrophe (')** `ar meid po-se-siv bai a-ding an a-pos-tro-fi (')` **to the end of the word.** `tu de end ov de werd.`

**Examples: candles--candles'; daughters--daughters';** `Eg-zam-pols: kan-dols--kan-dols'; do-ters--do-ters';` **mothers--mothers'; ships-- ships'; teachers--teachers'.** `ma-ders--ma-ders'; ships--ships'; ti-chers--ti-chers'.`

**Sentence: "Are you going to the Mothers'** `Sen-tens: "Ar iu go-ing tu de Ma-ders'` **and Daughters' dinner?"** `and Do-ters' di-ner?"`

**Plural nouns that do not end with "s"** `Plu-ral nauns dat du not end wid "s"` **are made possessive by adding an apostrophe (')** `ar meid po-se-siv bai a-ding an a-pos-tro-fi (')` **and an "s" to the end of the word.** `and an "s" tu de end ov de werd.`

**Examples: children--children's; men--men's;** `Eg-zam-pols: chil-dren--chil-drens; men--mens;` **people--people's; women--women's.** `pi-pol--pi-pols; wi-men--wi-mens.`

**Sentence: "This store specializes** `Sen-tens: "Dis stor spe-sha-lai-ses` **in children's clothing."** `in chil-drens klou-ding."`
