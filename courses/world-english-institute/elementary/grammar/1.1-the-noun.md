## INTRODUCTION TO THE NOUN

**In the Introductory Lesson,** `In de In-tro-dak-to-ri Le-son,` **we learned that all English words** `wi lernd dat ol Ing-glish words` **can be divided into eight "parts of speech":** `can bi di-vai-ded in-tu eit "parts ov spich":` **nouns, pronouns, adjectives, verbs,** `nauns, pro-nauns, a-dyek-tivs, verbs,` **adverbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and interjections.** `ad-verbs, pre-po-si-shons, con-yank-shons, and in-ter-yek-shons.` **Review the Introductory Lesson** `Ri-viu de In-tro-dak-to-ri Le-son` **until you are able to list** `an-til yu ar ei-bol tu list` **the eight parts of speech** `de eit parts ov spich` **and explain each one.** `and eks-plein ich wan.` **In this lesson, we begin** `In dis le-son, wi bi-gin` **our study of the noun.** `aur sta-di ov de naun.` **We will learn to recognize** `Wi wil lern tu re-cog-naiz` **and to use different kinds of nouns:** `and tu yus di-fe-rent kainds ov nauns:` **proper, common, collective, concrete,** `pro-per, co-mon, co-lek-tiv, con-crit,` **abstract, specific, and non-specific.** `abs-trakt, spe-ci-fik, and non-spe-ci-fik.` **Don't be afraid of these words.** `Dont bi a-freid ov diz words.` **You will understand their meaning** `Yu wil an-der-stand deir mi-ning` **by the time you finish this lesson.** `bai de taim yu fi-nish dis le-son.`

## NOUNS TO LEARN

**1. "cell" (noun)** `wan. "sel" (naun)` **- the smallest structural unit** `- de smo-lest strak-chu-ral yu-nit` **in the body of a plant or animal.** `in de bo-di ov a plant or a-ni-mal.`

**Sentence: "An animal cell is so small** `Sen-tens: "An a-ni-mal sel is so smol` **that we cannot see one** `dat wi ca-not si wan` **without a microscope."** `wi-daut a mai-cros-koup."`

**2. "evidence" (noun)** `tu. "e-vi-dens" (naun)` **- the facts on which** `- de fakts on wich` **a judgment can be made.** `a yadg-ment can bi meid.`

**Sentence: "Fossil records provide little or no evidence** `Sen-tens: "Fo-sil re-cords pro-vaid li-tol or no e-vi-dens` **to support the theory of evolution."** `tu sa-port de thi-o-ri ov e-vo-lu-shon."`

**3. "handiwork" (noun)** `thri. "han-di-werk" (naun)` **- the work that someone** `- de werk dat sam-wan` **has done by hand.** `has dan bai hand.`

**Sentence: "When we look at a sunset** `Sen-tens: "Wen wi luk at a san-set` **or a rainbow, we see** `or a rein-bou, wi si` **the handiwork of God."** `de han-di-werk ov God."`

**4. "mystery" (noun)** `for. "mis-te-ri" (naun)` **- something that is unknown** `- sam-thing dat is an-noun` **or unexplained.** `or a-neks-pleind.`

**Sentence: "There are many mysteries in nature** `Sen-tens: "Der ar me-ni mis-te-ris in nei-cher` **that scientists have not been able** `dat sai-en-tists hav not bin ei-bol` **to explain."** `tu eks-plein."`

**5. "personality" (noun)** `faiv. "per-so-na-li-ti" (naun)` **the qualities of being a person.** `de kwa-li-tis ov bi-ing a per-son.`

**Sentence: "God has all the qualities** `Sen-tens: "God has ol de kwa-li-tis` **of personality."** `ov per-so-na-li-ti."`

**6. "theory" (noun)** `siks. "thi-o-ri" (naun)` **- a system of conclusions** `- a sis-tem ov con-clu-shons` **based on reasoning.** `beist on ri-so-ning.`

**Sentence: "A theory is something a person believes** `Sen-tens: "A thi-o-ri is sam-thing a per-son bi-livs` **but is not able to prove.** `bat is not ei-bol tu pruv.`

**7. "universe" (noun)** `se-ven. "yu-ni-vers" (naun)` **- all things that exist,** `- ol things dat eg-sist,` **regarded as a whole.** `ri-gar-ded as a hol.`

**Sentence: "No one knows how big** `Sen-tens: "No wan nous hau big` **the universe really is."** `de yu-ni-vers ri-li is."`

## OTHER WORDS TO LEARN

**1. "create" (verb)** `wan. "cri-eit" (verb)` **- to cause to exist.** `- tu kos tu eg-sist.`

**Sentence: "In the beginning, God created** `Sen-tens: "In de bi-gi-ning, God cri-ei-ted` **the earth and sky."** `de erth and skai."`

**2. "design" (verb)** `tu. "di-sain" (verb)` **to create a plan, sketch, or pattern.** `tu cri-eit a plan, skech, or pa-tern.`

**Sentence: "Could any man have designed** `Sen-tens: "Kud e-ni man hav di-saind` **the human body?"** `de hiu-man bo-di?"`

**3. "evolve" (verb)** `thri. "i-volv" (verb)` **- to develop slowly.** `- tu di-ve-lop slou-li.`

**Sentence: "Some scientists believe that life on earth** `Sen-tens: "Sam sai-en-tists bi-liv dat laif on erth` **evolved spontaneously, without the help of God."** `i-volvd spon-tei-ni-os-li, wi-daut de help ov God."`

**4. "generate" (infinitive) [a verb preceded by "to"]** `for. "ye-ne-reit" (in-fi-ni-tiv) [a verb pri-si-ded bai "tu"]` **- to bring into existence.** `- tu bring in-tu eg-sis-tens.`

**Sentence: "Falling water is often used** `Sen-tens: "Fo-ling wo-ter is o-fen yusd` **to generate electricity."** `tu ye-ne-reit i-lek-tri-si-ti."`

**5. "reproductive" (adjective)** `faiv. "ri-pro-dak-tiv" (a-dyek-tiv)` **- serving to reproduce** `- ser-ving tu ri-pro-dus` **or to bring forth again.** `or tu bring forth a-gein.`

**Sentence: "The acorn is part of** `Sen-tens: "Di ei-korn is part ov` **the oak tree's reproductive system."** `di ouk tris ri-pro-dak-tiv sis-tem."`

**6. "spontaneously" (adverb)** `siks. "spon-tei-ni-os-li" (ad-verb)` **- without outside cause.** `- wi-daut aut-said kos.`

**Sentence: "Oily rags sometimes burst into flames** `Sen-tens: "Oi-li rags sam-taims berst in-tu fleims` **spontaneously."** `spon-tei-ni-os-li."`

## WHAT IS A NOUN?

**A noun is a word** `A naun is a werd` **that names a person, place, or thing** `dat neims a per-son, pleis, or thing` **(object, animal, idea, action, quality, feeling, or event).** `(ob-yekt, a-ni-mal, ai-di-a, ak-shon, kwa-li-ti, fi-ling, or i-vent).` **The term "noun" means "name."** `De term "naun" mins "neim."`

**All people have names.** `Ol pi-pol hav neims.` **You have a family name,** `Yu hav a fa-mi-li neim,` **a given name, and probably a middle name.** `a gi-ven neim, and pro-ba-bli a mi-dol neim.` **What are your names?** `Wat ar yor neims?` **Write them down.** `Rait dem daun.` **Each of your names is a noun.** `Ich ov yor neims is a naun.`

**You also have other names.** `Yu ol-so hav a-der neims.` **You are a student, a teacher,** `Yu ar a stu-dent, a ti-cher,` **a worker, a boy, girl, man, or woman.** `a wer-ker, a boi, gerl, man, or wu-man.` **You are a brother, sister, son, daughter,** `Yu ar a bro-der, sis-ter, son, do-ter,` **mother, or father.** `ma-der, or fa-der.` **These words are nouns.** `Diz werds ar nauns.` **They are names given to people.** `Dei ar neims gi-ven tu pi-pol.`

**Places, too, have names.** `Plei-ses, tu, hav neims.` **Where do you live?** `Wer du yu liv?` **On what street or road?** `On wat strit or roud?` **In what village, town, or city?** `In wat vi-lich, taun, or si-ti?` **In what county, state, province, or nation?** `In wat caun-ti, steit, pro-vins, or nei-shon?` **By what river or lake?** `Bai wat ri-ver or leik?` **These words are nouns also.** `Diz werds ar nauns ol-so.` **They are names of places.** `Dei ar neims ov plei-ses.`

**Things have names also.** `Things hav neims ol-so.` **Look around you.** `Luk a-raund yu.` **Make a list of the things you see.** `Meik a list ov de things yu si.` **The words that you write down are nouns.** `De werds dat yu rait daun ar nauns.` **Make a list of things** `Meik a list ov things` **you ate for breakfast and lunch today.** `yu eit for brek-fast and lanch tu-dei.` **You have just made another list of nouns.** `Yu hav yast meid a-na-der list ov nauns.` **Things like trees, grass, fish, and birds are nouns.** `Things laik tris, gras, fish, and berds ar nauns.` **So are qualities like wisdom, power, and truth.** `So ar kwa-li-tis laik wis-dom, pau-er, and truth.` **So are feelings such as fear, love, happiness,** `So ar fi-lings sach as fir, lov, ha-pi-nes,` **and joy, and events such as** `and yoi, and i-vents sach as` **the creation, a meeting, a vacation, or a party.** `de cri-ei-shon, a mi-ting, a va-kei-shon, or a par-ti.` **All these words are nouns.** `Ol diz werds ar nauns.` **They are names of things.** `Dei ar neims ov things.`

| **mother** `ma-der`         | **nursery** `ner-se-ri` | **diaper** `dai-per` |
| **president** `pre-si-dent` | **company** `kam-pa-ni` | **profit** `pro-fit` |

**Sometimes a noun is a group of words.** `Sam-taims a naun is a grup ov werds.`

**Examples: "maid of honor," "justice of the peace,"** `Eg-sam-pols: "meid ov o-nor," "yas-tis ov de pis,"` **"Mount Hood," "seagull," "ice cream."** `"Maunt Hud," "si-gal," "ais krim."` **Since these words must go together** `Sins diz werds mast go tu-ge-der` **to make sense, each group of words** `tu meik sens, ich grup ov werds` **is considered one noun.** `is con-si-derd wan naun.` **"Ice cream" is one noun.** `"Ais krim" is wan naun.`

## PROPER, COMMON, AND COLLECTIVE NOUNS

**A "proper noun" names a specific person,** `A "pro-per naun" neims a spe-si-fik per-son,` **place, or thing and always begins** `pleis, or thing and ol-weis bi-gins` **with a capital letter.** `wid a ca-pi-tal le-ter.` **Examples: Adam, Tigris, Beijing, China, Monday,** `Eg-sam-pols: A-dam, Tai-gris, Bei-ying, Chai-na, Man-dei,` **July, English. The first word in a sentence** `Yu-lai, Ing-glish. De ferst werd in a sen-tens` **also starts with a capital letter.** `ol-so starts wid a ca-pi-tal le-ter.`

**Write the names of people in your family.** `Rait de neims ov pi-pol in yor fa-mi-li.` **These names are proper nouns.** `Diz neims ar pro-per nauns.` **Examples: John, Eve.** `Eg-sam-pols: Yon, Iv.`

**Names of your street, city, state or province,** `Neims ov yor strit, si-ti, steit or pro-vins,` **and nation are proper nouns.** `and nei-shon ar pro-per nauns.` **Example: 15 Oak Street, Cole, Iowa 97030; U.S.A.** `Eg-sam-pol: Fif-tin Ouk Strit, Koul, Ai-o-wa Nain-ti Se-ven Thau-sand Ther-ti; Yu Es Ei.`

**Make a list of mountains, rivers, lakes,** `Meik a list ov maun-tins, ri-vers, leiks,` **seas, or oceans in your area.** `sis, or o-shons in yor e-ri-a.` **Each of these words is a proper noun** `Ich ov diz werds is a pro-per naun` **and should begin with a capital letter.** `and shud bi-gin wid a ca-pi-tal le-ter.` **Examples: Mount Everest, Nile River, Dead Sea.** `Eg-sam-pols: Maunt E-ve-rest, Nail Ri-ver, Ded Si.`

**A "common noun" is a word** `A "co-mon naun" is a werd` **that names any kind of person, place, or thing.** `dat neims e-ni kaind ov per-son, pleis, or thing.` **Examples: man, woman, river, lake, city, country, day, month, language.** `Eg-sam-pols: man, wu-man, ri-ver, leik, si-ti, kan-tri, dei, manth, lang-guich.` **A common noun never begins with a capital letter** `A co-mon naun ne-ver bi-gins wid a ca-pi-tal le-ter` **except at the beginning of a sentence** `ek-sept at de bi-gi-ning ov a sen-tens` **or when used with a proper noun.** `or wen yusd wid a pro-per naun.` **The words "river" and "college" are common nouns,** `De werds "ri-ver" and "co-lich" ar co-mon nauns,` **but when they are used to specify** `bat wen dei ar yusd tu spe-si-fai` **a certain river or college,** `a ser-ten ri-ver or co-lich,` **they become proper nouns and are capitalized.** `dei bi-kam pro-per nauns and ar ca-pi-ta-laizd.` **Examples: Columbia River; Columbia Christian College.** `Eg-sam-pols: Ko-lam-bi-a Ri-ver; Ko-lam-bi-a Kris-chan Co-lich.` **Whenever a common noun is used** `We-ne-ver a co-mon naun is yusd` **to set a person, place, or thing apart** `tu set a per-son, pleis, or thing a-part` **from others of the same class,** `from a-ders ov de seim klas,` **it becomes a proper noun.** `it bi-kams a pro-per naun.`

**A "collective noun" refers to a group** `A "ko-lek-tiv naun" ri-fers tu a grup` **of people or things that are bound together** `ov pi-pol or things dat ar baund tu-ge-der` **as a unit.** `as a yu-nit.` **A collective noun is singular in form** `A ko-lek-tiv naun is sing-gyu-lar in form` **but refers to more than one.** `bat ri-fers tu mor dan wan.`

**Examples: assembly, crew, fleet, flock, herd, jury, litter, swarm, school.** `Eg-sam-pols: a-sem-bli, kru, flit, flok, herd, yu-ri, li-ter, sworm, skul.`

## CONCRETE AND ABSTRACT NOUNS

**A "concrete noun" names a person, place, or thing** `A "kon-krit naun" neims a per-son, pleis, or thing` **that can be heard, seen, smelled, tasted, or touched.** `dat can bi herd, sin, smeld, teis-ted, or tacht.`

**Examples: owl, banana, cake, train, oil, pig, man.** `Eg-sam-pols: aul, ba-na-na, keik, trein, oil, pig, man.`

**We become aware of these persons and material objects** `Wi bi-kam a-wer ov diz per-sons and ma-ti-ri-al ob-yekts` **through the physical senses.** `thru de fi-si-kal sen-ses.`

**An "abstract noun" names a quality, feeling, or idea.** `An "abs-trakt naun" neims a kwa-li-ti, fi-ling, or ai-di-a.`

**Examples: love, fear, intelligence, goodness.** `Eg-sam-pols: lov, fir, in-te-li-yens, gud-nes.`

**These are general.** `Diz ar ye-ne-ral.` **They do not refer to a certain case** `Dei du not ri-fer tu a ser-ten keis` **or example or to a material object.** `or eg-zam-pol or tu a ma-ti-ri-al ob-yekt.` **We become aware of them** `Wi bi-kam a-wer ov dem` **through our thoughts and emotions.** `thru aur thots and i-mo-shons.`

| **flower** `flau-er` | **beauty** `biu-ti`     |
| **joke** `yok`       | **humor** `hiu-mor`     |

## SPECIFIC AND NONSPECIFIC NOUNS

**A "specific noun" is a noun** `A "spe-si-fik naun" is a naun` **that names a certain class of persons or things.** `dat neims a ser-ten klas ov per-sons or things.`

**A nonspecific noun is general in nature** `A non-spe-si-fik naun is ye-ne-ral in nei-cher` **and does not specify a certain class** `and das not spe-si-fai a ser-ten klas` **of person or things.** `ov per-son or things.`
